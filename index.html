OVERVIEW

The goal of this project is to repurpose motion capture technologies by working with historically marginalized communities to create visual sound media narratives that emerge from movement data.

DETAILS

The Current State of Motion Capture Technology: Who is being excluded? [The Problem]

When we typically think of motion/movement capture, we think of CGI films like 'Planet of the Apes'. While it is great for the entertainment and performance industry, this technology only serves few, leaving a large gap in representation.

In this project, we ask: WHO is being excluded? And HOW can we use this technology in a way that is more accessible, inclusive and ethical?

Decentralizing the Use of Mo-Cap Technology [The Solution]

Traditionally, mo-cap technology is costly and primarily used in Hollywood studios. We, on the other hand, have been beta-testing a new mobile motion capture technology that affords us the ability to reach and collect the everyday movements of those who are not traditionally seen in the media. In this way, we are rendering the invisible visible and using mo-cap as a technology of resistance.

Movement as Individualized "Fingerprints": Why capture movement at all? [Explaination]

Movement, while often overlooked, is a major part of our identities. In fact, we each have our own unique movement "fingerprints" that tell the story of who we are.

We are able to use mo-cap technology to reveal and express these patterns of movement in cultural contexts, which ultimately gives us a better understanding of how movement is passed down generationally.

In other words, we are using this technology to move away from "dominant stories" or what normally/traditionally seen -- and looking at different communities - and their everyday movements.

How Mo-Cap Becomes a Technology of Resistance [Execution/Result]

The result of our movement portrait collection is twofold: The first is to create an art exhibition that conveys ethnographic "movement portraits", or composites of movement data with narrative stories engrained in everyday movements. The second is a digital movement repository or archive as a open source resource for researchers, artists and educators worldwide.

In these ways, we are using mo-cap technology, a relatively new tool, as speculative technology, paving the path for future motion capture work that is more accessible, ethical, decentralized, and inclusive [**Side note - ji - before more commercialized, profit driven entities get there first].
